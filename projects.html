Remote Sensing

Remote sensing is based on the understanding that an image comprises a collection of frequency components. Our collective experience interpreting remote sensing imagery can determine how image filtering can implement imaging functions that pass, filter, enhance, or suppress various frequencies. The objective of image filtering strategies is to locate the desired environmental information of interest in certain parts of the frequency spectrum or to remove undesirable frequencies.

The use of Spectral properties analysis allows our team to identify a very wide mixture of natural and anthropogenic land covers and land uses. With our image acquisitions spanning almost 4 years, we explore the spectral signatures of anthropogenic landscape changes spanning these years, and analyse impacts on the environment. We are able to make complex decisions comparing three distinct phenological periods, Spring (leaf-off), summer (full foliage) and fall (senescent and leaf-off) by exploring the seasonal/phenological spectral expressions of the natural and agricultural landscape components.
