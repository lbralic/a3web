Meet Our Team

Meghan Noonan is a Perth based GIS consultant specializing in spatial analysis and programming. Prior to starting at GeomSolutions Meghan completed her MSc at the Memorial University where her research focused on modelling forest restoration scenarios after moose overbrowsing. Much of her research utilized aerial imagery analysis and model building which led her to develop a passion for GIS. She has now been working with GeomSolutions for five years where she has worked on a variety of projects including capturing drone imagery of conservation land, building spatial models to analyze deforestation trends, and using LANDSAT imagery to classify land use. GeomSolutions has a fantastic team, and we look forward to working with you on your next project! See Lucija's linkedin and personal website for more information.

Lucija Bralic is a GIS Analyst at GeomSolutions. Lucija began working at GeomSolutions in June 2020 as a GIS Technician. The following June, she joined the office team and has played a vital role in various projects, which include wildlife monitoring, risk assessments, and ecological restoration projects. With a background in Environmental Biology, Lucija leverages her scientific knowledge and GIS experience to provide the best possible services for GeomSolutions’ clients. See Lucija's personal website for more information.

Kevin Van Dijk began working with GeomSolutions in 2018, supporting the team’s mission to provide environmental support services using GIS technology. His background in Conservation Biology ensures that professional environmental solutions are achieved using the latest location intelligence and technology. With a passion for environmental solutions and creative problem-solving, Kevin is confident GeomSolutions will help your team to benefit from our experience with the latest innovations in GIS technology.

Jessica Murdoch has been a GIS Analyst with GeomSolutions for just over a year now, where she has been able to use her previous experience to provide outstanding service to our customers. Before becoming a member of the team last year, she completed a MSc at York University where her research was focused on exploring associations between cyanobacteria blooms and the related environmental factors that are causing mesotrophic and oligotrophic lakes in Ontario to be experiencing an increase in blooms. This research utilized different spatial analysis techniques, database creation and management and programming to customize geographical information. Jessica has quickly become a valuable member of the team with a multi-disciplinary background and her passion for environmental monitoring.
